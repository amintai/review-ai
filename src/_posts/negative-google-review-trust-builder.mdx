## The "Silent Majority" is Watching
When you reply to a negative review, you aren't just talking to the angry customer. You are talking to the **hundreds of potential customers** who will read that interaction later. 

Research shows that businesses that respond to negative reviews are perceived as more trustworthy than those with only "perfect" 5-star ratings and no engagement.

## The 4-Step De-escalation Framework

1. **Acknowledgment & Speed:** Respond within 24 hours. Silence is seen as indifference.
2. **The "Heart-First" Apology:** Even if the customer is wrong, apologize for their *experience*. "We're sorry to hear your visit didn't meet our standards."
3. **Specific Context:** Address Mentioned issues (e.g., "the wait time," "the cold soup"). This proves a human read it.
4. **Offline Resolution:** Move the sensitive details to private. "Please reach out to our manager at manager@business.com so we can make this right."
